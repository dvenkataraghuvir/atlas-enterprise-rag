ON-CALL AND INCIDENT ESCALATION POLICY
Document Owner: Site Reliability Engineering
Last Updated: Q4 2023

1. ON-CALL ROTATION

All engineers on the SRE and Platform teams participate in a weekly on-call rotation managed in PagerDuty. Each rotation consists of a primary and a secondary on-call engineer.

On-call schedules are published two weeks in advance. Schedule swaps require 48 hours notice and must be recorded in PagerDuty.

2. ESCALATION LADDER

When an incident page fires:

Step 1 — Primary on-call is paged immediately via PagerDuty (phone + app notification).
Step 2 — If unacknowledged for 8 minutes, the secondary on-call is paged.
Step 3 — If still unacknowledged after 15 minutes total, the Engineering Manager on the incident rotation is paged.
Step 4 — If unacknowledged after 20 minutes, the VP of Engineering is notified.

3. SEVERITY LEVELS

Sev-1: Customer-facing outage or data loss. Bypasses the standard escalation ladder. Automatically pages the Incident Commander (IC) rotation in parallel and opens a dedicated war-room Slack channel.

Sev-2: Significant service degradation affecting >10% of customers. Follows the standard ladder. Requires a status page update within 30 minutes.

Sev-3: Isolated bug or performance issue with limited customer impact. Normal business hours response. No on-call page required unless it escalates.

4. INCIDENT COMMANDER RESPONSIBILITIES

The Incident Commander (IC) is responsible for:
- Declaring the severity level
- Coordinating response across teams
- Updating the status page every 30 minutes during active Sev-1 incidents
- Calling the all-clear when the incident is resolved

IC rotation is separate from the on-call engineering rotation. A list of current ICs is maintained in the #incidents Slack channel.

5. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

A blameless post-incident review (PIR) is required for all Sev-1 and Sev-2 incidents.

PIR due date: within 3 business days of incident resolution.

The PIR must include:
- Timeline of events
- Root cause analysis
- Action items with owners and due dates

PIR documents are stored in the Confluence space "Engineering / Incidents".
